Photosynthesis - Class 10 Science Notes

Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants make their own food using sunlight, carbon dioxide, and water. Plants are called autotrophs because they can produce their own food.

Chloroplasts contain chlorophyll, the green pigment that absorbs light energy. The process consists of light-dependent reactions (which require sunlight) and light-independent or dark reactions (which do not require sunlight directly).

The overall balanced equation for photosynthesis is:
6CO2 + 6H2O -> C6H12O6 + 6O2

Factors affecting photosynthesis include light intensity, carbon dioxide concentration, and temperature. As light intensity increases, the rate of photosynthesis increases until it reaches a plateau.
